# Bias-Variance Tradeoff

Bias and variance are two major sources of prediction error.

## Bias

High bias means the model is too simple.

Characteristics:

- Underfitting
- Poor training performance
- Poor testing performance

---

## Variance

High variance means the model is too complex.

Characteristics:

- Overfitting
- Excellent training performance
- Poor testing performance

---

## Tradeoff

As model complexity increases:

- Bias decreases
- Variance increases

The goal is to find a balance that minimizes overall error.

---

## Comparison

| High Bias | High Variance |
|------------|---------------|
| Underfitting | Overfitting |
| Simple Model | Complex Model |
| Misses Patterns | Learns Noise |

---

## Reducing Bias

- More complex models
- Better features
- More training

## Reducing Variance

- More data
- Regularization
- Dropout
- Early Stopping

---

## Key Takeaway

A good model balances bias and variance to generalize well.
